Colour Paradigms and Accessibility

In a globalised world where people from all across the globe can access the internet, it is important to ensure coders understand the significance of certain colours in certain regions. In addition, accessibility should be prioritised by making smart colour choices.

Colour Paradigms

Across the world, different colours have different meanings.

Red

In Chinese culture, red symbolises luck and happiness and is worn during New Year to ward off evil.

In South Africa, red is a colour of mourning and bloodshed and is used at funerals.

In the west, red symbolises love and is used in romantic gestures.

Orange

In Hinduism, saffron and orange are sacred, as they represent fire that burns away impurities.

In Ukraine, the colour orange symbolises strength and is a reminder of the 2004 Orange Revolution that was caused by electoral fraud.

In the Middle East, orange is a colour that represents mourning and is used at funerals.

Yellow

Yellow is a colour that is cheery and represents warmth.

However, yellow is also a colour associated with danger and warning.

In Japan, yellow chrysanthemum represent the royal family.

Green

In Ireland, green is associated with good luck, leprechauns, and Saint Patrick.

Green can represent jealousy.

In Islam, green is a sacred colour, and is associated with paradise in the Quran.

Blue

In the west, the colour blue is associated with royalty, with the phrase 'blue-blooded' referring to people of nobility.

Blue is a colour that can be associated with depression and sadness.

In Germany, blue is associated with drunkenness, with the phrase “blau sein” meaning being drunk.

Purple

In Thailand and Brazil, purple is a colour of mourning.

Generally, purple is a colour associated with royalty.

In America, purple is a colour of strength and bravery.

Colour Accessibility

When making a website, colour accessibility is important as it allows users to use the sites without straining their eyes.

Using pure black against pure white makes readers need to strain their eyes more due to the brightness polarity.
